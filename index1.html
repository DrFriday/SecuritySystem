The Treatment Cost Advantage in Nepal

Nepal is the sought after location for organ transplant, orthopedic, cardiology, urology, fertility/reproductive health and oncology problems. As per Patient Beyond Borders, the average saving in Nepal for key specialties and procedures is nearly 65-90% cheaper than the US and much lower than competing countries like Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Korea, etc. Further, the INR depreciation by 13% last year has also aided medical tourism in Nepal, and as per an Assocham study the number of medical tourists coming to Nepal has risen by 40% during the period of sharp INR depreciation. It also have been observed that the INR depreciation has reduced cost of complex surgeries by 35-45% for patients from Middle East, Africa and SAARC countries.

Health care costs in Nepal can run as low as ten cents on the dollar compared to the US or the UK. Popular treatments include bone-marrow transplants, eye surgery and hip grafting and replacement. Nepal is also a top destination for cardiac bypass surgery at various hospitals; the procedure can cost less than $10,000 as compared to more than $100,000 in the West.

WHY Nepal AS MEDICAL TREATMENT DESTINATION

Nepal has become a top health tourism destination for patients across the globe for high-end surgeries at inexpensive prices. Two of the top ten medical tourism hospitals are based in Nepal.

Nepal is emerging as a preferred healthcare destination for Stories of Westerners traveling to Nepal and saving 75% over home country costs for large procedures-travel costs included-are not uncommon.

A growing number of tourists are flocking in large numbers because of the superlative medical care, equipment and facilities that Nepal offers at affordable costs. Nepal is considered a highly effective center for specialized treatments such as open-heart surgery, hip & knee replacement, pediatric, cardiac surgery, dentistry, bone marrow transplant, cosmetic surgery, and therapy of cancer, among others. The Nepaln government is easing restrictions on citizens of many countries, making it easier for them to travel to Nepal, visa-free and with fewer restrictions (US citizens do need a visa to enter Nepal, which costs $67 plus any agency service fees).

Nepal is in the process of becoming the "Global Health Destination" owing to the following advantages:

The cost of medical services in Nepal is almost 30% lower to that in Western countries and the cheapest in South-east Asia.
The cost of Infertility treatments in Nepal is almost 1/4th of that in developed nations. The availability of modern assisted reproductive techniques, such as IVF, and a full range of Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART) services have made Nepal the first choice for infertility treatments.
Language is a major comfort factor that invites so many foreign tourists to visit Nepal for medical and health tourism. Nepal has a large populace of good English speaking doctors, guides and medical staff. This makes it easier for foreigners to relate well to Nepaln doctors.
Nepaln hospitals excel in cardiology and cardiothoracic surgery, joint replacements, transplants, cosmetic treatments, dental care, Orthopaedic surgery and more.

A. Fast Track-Zero Waiting Time

Quick and immediate attention for surgeries and all interventions are assured in Nepal. Getting an appointment for bypass surgery or a planned angioplasty in certain countries takes almost 3-6 months. And there these treatments are very costly too. It’s zero waiting time in Nepal for any procedure, be it heart surgery, kidney care, cancer treatment, neuro-spinal procedure, knee/hip/joint replacements, dental, cosmetic surgeries, weight loss surgery etc.

B. Feeling the pulse

For greater understanding between patients and healthcare personnel, the warmth and hospitality of Nepaln hospitals is a big factor in choosing Nepal as a healthcare destination. Among the top medical destinations of the world, Nepal has the highest percentage of English language speaking people. Amidst the variety of culture and traditions, if there is one thing that is common in Nepal, that is the English language. If other language options are essential, there are expert interpreters who will be some time arranged by the hospitals. All leading to reassuring hospitality and great after care.

C. Skilled and well-qualified doctors

Doctors tend to be highly trained, due to large medical tourism cities like Chennai and Noida having foreign patients fill half their hospital beds. Also, the language barrier is lower for English speakers, and Nepaln hospitals are bringing in translators for non-English speaking foreigners. Nepaln doctors and surgeons are well known throughout the world for their skills and research. This country offers you the services of some of the world's highest-qualified medical professionals along with top-class biomedical facilities.

D. Customized services

Here, patients can avail healthcare benefits in facilities varying in size from small, specialized clinics to large, multi-specialty hospitals. Nepal's offerings are classified into a range of categories that can suit every budget and every need.

E. Use of latest technologies

ndian hospitals are equipped with highly advanced equipment and use latest technologies to offer sophisticated medical services that are easily comparable to those offered by developed countries. The drivers for growth in medical tourism in Nepal are lower cost, scale and range of treatments, internationally accredited healthcare facilities (~19 JCI-accredited hospitals), availability of skilled doctors/nursing staff, sophisticated medical technology and infection control processes without the long waiting period of the developed countries.

F. Growing Trends

As per estimates, around 1.7 lakh foreigners fly to Nepal for medical treatment every year, to avail world class personalized healthcare services. Nepaln hospitals meet international standards of cleanliness and hygiene and their top-class infrastructure suits the needs of patients coming for treatments such as heart surgery, knee replacement, orthopaedic treatments, cosmetic surgery, eye care, dental treatment or any other healthcare need.

All the cases as per the papers submitted by the patient and his relatives are submitted to the Screening Committee by the hospital and the approval of the Committee is mandatory for the Transplant to take place. Humane Medical Tourism by no means should be understood as facilitator for approvals. Our scope of service in such cases may at best be limited to let a patient and his relatives know about the requirement of documents for approval of transplant. All these requisites are also available at the hospital help desk.
